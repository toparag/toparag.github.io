🎯 Core Strategy

My approach revolves around high-time-frame Fair Value Gaps (FVGs) leading to continuation models.

  1. Determine market bias (bullish/bearish) using 1D, 4H, and 1H charts.
  2. After determining bias, look for retracement at the equilibrium of the range overlapping a 15m+ FVG.
  3. Once the FVG rebalances, look for an IFVG in the direction of the bias.
  4. Target internal liquidity (HTF FVGs) or external liquidity (major highs/lows).
  5. Aim for 1+ risk-to-reward ratio.

💡 Key Concepts

🏦 Liquidity

Liquidity is where the money lies — swing highs and lows where stop losses accumulate. Markets clear these before trending further.

⚖️ Fair Value Gap (FVG)

Represents imbalance via a 3-candle formation. Price revisits these gaps to fill orders left unfilled in prior moves.

🔄 Inverse FVG (IFVG)

Occurs when price fills and balances a previous FVG — often confirming continuation.

📉 SMT Divergence

When correlated assets diverge — e.g., NQ takes a high but ES doesn’t — signaling potential reversals or exhaustion.

🧮 Risk Management

Simple but effective capital protection rules I follow:

  • Risk 1% MAX per trade on live accounts; for prop challenges, risk 12.5–25% of available drawdown; funded stage: 5–12.5%.
  • If first trade wins, stop for the day. If first trade loses, one more high-probability trade at 50% risk size — then stop.
  • Always use stop-loss and take-profit.
  • Trade strictly between 8 PM – 10 PM (Asia/overnight session).

📝 Real Trade Examples + Journaling

Journaling transforms every trade into a learning opportunity. I record entries for each trade: setup, bias, confluences, PnL, emotions, and lessons.

This helps track behavior, recognize emotional triggers, and focus on process over outcome — making each trade a lesson in discipline and improvement.

🧠 Trading Psychology

Psychology separates consistent traders from impulsive ones. It’s about mastering patience, confidence, and emotional balance.

  • Maintain composure during drawdowns.
  • Avoid revenge trading and overtrading after losses.
  • Respect your system — every loss is part of the game.
  • “The best traders think in probabilities.” — Mark Douglas

📘 Lessons & Key Takeaways

  • Discipline > Motivation: Discipline sustains progress long after motivation fades.
  • Process > Outcome: Judge your decision-making by rule adherence, not PnL.
  • Input > Output: Focus on what you can control; ignore what you can’t.
  • Losses = Lessons: Every loss is valuable if analyzed and learned from.
  • Patience Pays: Only take A+ setups — quality over quantity.
